Modelling neuron morphology

Experimental data about neuron morphology in many species is available through the NeuroMorpho database [1].

NetMorph is a C++ environment to simulate neuronal morphogenesis from the perspective of the individual growth cone within axons or dendrites, covering elongation, branching and turning.In this way, neurons with realistic axonal and dendritic morphologies, including neurite curvature, can be generated. Synapses are formed as neurons grow out and axonal and dendritic branches come in close proximity of each other [2]. Compared to CX3D, NetMorph is computationally less expensive, making it useful for theoretical studies and simulating larger networks; on the other hand, NetMorprh is less flexible than CX3D [3].

Modelling axon growth

Matlab script for spatial network growth. For details on the algorithm, look at Kaiser and Hilgetag (2004) (PDF, [4])

Matlab code for simulating the growth of axons and connection establishment can be found here. For axonal growth simulations in two or three dimensions, run devsim2d and devsim3d, respectively [5].

All previous routines assume that axons grow in a straight line. To test the scenario that axons randomly change direction, we can look at the effect that this would have on overall axon lengths and axon trajectories. Code for modelling curved axonal pathways can be found here and is described in [6].
